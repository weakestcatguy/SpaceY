Communications
Built with the intent for eventual interstellar travel, Voyager 2 included a large, 3.7 m (12 ft) parabolic, high-gain antenna (see diagram) to transceive data via the Deep Space Network on Earth. Communications are conducted over the S-band (about 13 cm wavelength) and X-band (about 3.6 cm wavelength), providing data rates of up to 115.2 kilobits per second at the distance of Jupiter; this rates decreases according to inverse-square law as it travels farther away from Earth.[18] When the spacecraft is out of line-of-sight and unable to communicate, a digital tape recorder (DTR) can record about 64 megabytes of data for transmission at another time.[19]
