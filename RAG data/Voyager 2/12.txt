Encounter with Uranus
Further information: Exploration of Uranus
The closest approach to Uranus occurred on January 24, 1986, when Voyager 2 came within 81,500 km (50,600 mi) of the planet's cloudtops.[51] Voyager 2 also discovered 11 previously unknown moons: Cordelia, Ophelia, Bianca, Cressida, Desdemona, Juliet, Portia, Rosalind, Belinda, Puck and Perdita.[B] The mission also studied the planet's unique atmosphere, caused by its axial tilt of 97.8°, and examined the Uranian ring system.[51] The length of a day on Uranus as measured by Voyager 2 is 17 hours, 14 minutes.[51] Uranus was shown to have a magnetic field that was misaligned with its rotational axis, unlike other planets that had been visited to that point,[52][55] and a helix-shaped magnetic tail stretching 10 million km (6.2 million mi) away from the Sun.[52]
